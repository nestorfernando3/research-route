# Researcher profile

Use `RESEARCHER.md` as a versioned epistemic and authorial instrument, never as a clinical assessment or biography-as-proof. Ask only what the project needs, explain why, and let the researcher decline.

## Disclose the privacy paths first

Before asking any question that could elicit sensitive experience, identity, health, trauma, relationships, political belief, or other private material, tell the researcher:

1. **Hosted/external chat:** answer here only with content they authorize as `operational` or `publishable`. They must not paste a `private` answer. They may self-record it in a researcher-controlled local location that the agent cannot access, then provide only a researcher-authored operational consequence they explicitly authorize—for example, “Prioritize institutional mechanisms; do not request personal motivation.”
2. **Truly local/offline harness:** it may read or process a private answer only after the researcher explicitly authorizes that harness, purpose, fields, and duration. Keep the private record local and out of prompts to external services, handoffs, and manuscripts.

Confirm which path applies and whether the researcher wants to skip the sensitive question. A hosted/external agent never asks to inspect the private record, infer its contents from the operational consequence, or verify why the consequence matters.

## Interview one question at a time

After the disclosure, ask one question, record and classify only the content authorized for this harness, confirm the classification, then continue. Adapt the language to the researcher. Cover:

1. Which intellectual, disciplinary, cultural, or interpretive traditions shape how you approach this question?
2. Which values or commitments should the inquiry preserve, test, or place under tension?
3. Which relevant experiences or forms of expertise may orient your attention? Do not request intimate detail that the project does not need.
4. What kinds of evidence or explanation do you trust most, and which tend to leave you unconvinced?
5. How much ambiguity, contradiction, or unresolved plurality should the paper preserve?
6. Which recurring intuitions, sensitivities, or conceptual attractions are guiding you now?
7. Which blind spots, confirmation risks, disciplinary limits, or excluded standpoints should the work actively test?
8. Which unresolved tensions are productive rather than problems to eliminate?
9. What evidence, counterargument, interpretive failure, or ethical concern would change your position?
10. Which writing samples, if any, may be used to infer voice? Specify who may inspect them and for what purpose.

For every response, require exactly one permission label: `private | operational | publishable`. In hosted/external chat, `private` means “self-record locally and do not send”; the agent records only an authorized operational consequence, if the researcher chooses to provide one.

- `private`: remains in a researcher-controlled local record; hosted/external agents never read it, and it never enters handoffs, external prompts, or manuscripts.
- `operational`: may guide authorized work and privacy-safe handoffs; never becomes publication text.
- `publishable`: is eligible for a later reflexive-position proposal, but is not automatically approved manuscript prose.

When one response contains material with different permissions, the researcher separates it before transmission. Record only the wording authorized for the active harness; do not infer sensitive facts or upgrade a label. If the researcher wants to reconsider a private answer for publication, they relabel it locally and provide only the newly `publishable` content. Record that permission change without requesting or comparing the former private version. Relabeling still does not approve manuscript wording.

## Initial approval checkpoint

Draft profile version `v1` in `RESEARCHER.md` with date, scope, each transmitted response's `operational` or `publishable` label, authorized operational consequences, change conditions, and authorized samples. In a hosted/external workflow, leave private content absent; do not summarize it. Show the visible profile and ask the researcher to approve, amend, relabel, or delete it. Do not use the profile operationally until this checkpoint is recorded in `DECISIONS.md` and linked from `ROUTE.md`.

## Compare versions without diagnosis

At a material checkpoint or project close:

1. Compare only the fields authorized and visible to the active harness. Never request the researcher's local private version for comparison.
2. Record `previous position`, `current position`, `evidence or encounter associated with change`, `what remains unresolved`, and `researcher wording/approval`.
3. Describe changed positions, evidentiary preferences, questions, or tensions. Do not assign motives, personality traits, pathologies, identity essences, or causal diagnoses.
4. Preserve both versions and link the comparison; never rewrite the past version to make the trajectory appear cleaner.

## Propose reflexive positioning

Only propose reflexive text when positionality is methodologically or interpretively relevant. Create a review block with:

```markdown
### Reflexive-position proposal

- relevance:
- approved content:
- claim supported:
- risk:
- researcher approval: pending | approved | rejected | revise
```

Explain what the positioning lets a reader assess—for example, access, interpretive standpoint, relationship to participants or archive, or a limitation. Biography does not establish the truth of a substantive claim. Keep evidence and inference independently traceable.

Before transforming eligible `publishable` content, show the exact proposed wording in its manuscript context and obtain explicit researcher approval. Approval of profile collection, local permission relabeling, or a `publishable` label does not satisfy this second checkpoint.

## Red flags

- Never diagnose the researcher or psychologize a change in view.
- Never essentialize a person, community, identity, discipline, or tradition.
- Never request or read `private` content in a hosted/external workflow, or expose locally authorized private content in a handoff, manuscript, or external tool.
- Never use biography as evidence for a scholarly claim.
- Never publish, paraphrase, or allude to profile content without the required approval.

Counter the rationalization “the next agent needs the personal story”: it needs only the researcher-authored, authorized operational consequence. Counter “publishable means permission to insert”: it means eligible for a separate contextualized approval, not approved prose.
