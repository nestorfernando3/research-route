# Research Route

Build a rigorous, portable academic-paper project whose evidence, decisions, open questions, and next action survive changes of session, agent, or harness.

## Core contract

- Keep critical state in the project files, not only in chat or memory. Treat `ROUTE.md` as canonical and `HANDOFF.md` as a session snapshot that never overrides it.
- Advance discovery and manuscript composition together through linked claims, evidence, decisions, and prose.
- Let evidence reopen the venue, thesis, method, corpus, interpretation, or scope.
- Store concise, auditable rationales rather than hidden reasoning.
- Calibrate rigor to risk and stop when more work has low expected decision value.
- Protect researcher agency, privacy, source integrity, rights, and institutional obligations.

## Start or resume

For a new project, copy `assets/route-template/` into an empty project directory and complete the initial state with the researcher.

For an existing project, read `ROUTE.md` and `HANDOFF.md` first. Use them to identify the destination, settled constraints, live work, blocks, and exact next action. Open only linked artifacts needed for the present objective. If canonical state conflicts with the handoff, follow `ROUTE.md` and flag the discrepancy.

Before substantive work, confirm the session's one primary intellectual objective and whether the relevant work is exploratory or publication-bound.

## Three cycles

- **Discover:** investigate the researcher position, questions, literature, materials, rivals, methods, and provisional venue.
- **Argue:** form claims, link evidence, expose inference, test alternatives, and calibrate the contribution.
- **Compose:** design and revise architecture, bilingual voice, argument movement, and editorial fit.

Move between cycles whenever new evidence or review exposes a material weakness. Keep the current cycle and its consequences visible in `ROUTE.md`.

## Human checkpoints

Obtain explicit researcher approval for the initial epistemic profile, provisional target venue, first defensible contribution, consequential method/corpus/interpretive strategy, stabilized thesis, any conversion of private material into publishable positioning, and the proposed submission version.

Between checkpoints, proceed autonomously with reversible work inside the approved scope. Record the decision and a concise rationale in the canonical project state.

## Choose the next action

Choose the unblocked action most likely to reduce an important uncertainty relative to its cost. Prefer work that can change a consequential decision, unblock later work, or resolve a live contradiction. State a stopping condition before open-ended searching or revision.

Keep uncertain concerns in `Fog` until they can be formulated precisely. Create a separate work item only when independent traceability, delegation, coordination, or a reusable artifact justifies it.

## Close a session

Save outputs in their canonical homes; update affected claims, decisions, contradictions, fog, blocks, budget, and frontier; then update `ROUTE.md`. Complete `HANDOFF.md` with both mechanical state and the intellectual change, invalidated assumptions, live contradiction, researcher decisions needed, and exact next action with its reason.

Before transfer, check that another agent can continue from the local files without chat history and that private profile material is excluded unless explicitly authorized.

## Load references

Load only the detailed reference needed for the current objective:

- `references/researcher-profile.md` for profile, privacy, reflexive positioning, and researcher change.
- `references/venue-fingerprint.md` for venue choice, sampling, fit, and retargeting.
- `references/research-and-claims.md` for searching, source integrity, claims, contribution testing, adversarial work, budgets, and stopping.
- `references/writing-and-review.md` for bilingual voice, composition, audits, review simulations, and submission preparation.

If a referenced file is not yet present, preserve the contract above and do not invent its detailed procedure.

## Red flags

- Do not fabricate or silently complete citations, pages, quotations, findings, source content, or access levels.
- Do not claim complete novelty, publication certainty, or venue fit without adequate testing.
- Do not mark a venue fingerprint complete with fewer than ten representative articles read at full-text level.
- Do not treat objections as cosmetic; allow adverse evidence to change the project.
- Do not expose private researcher material or transform it into publishable prose without explicit approval.
- Do not collapse distinct concepts during Spanish-English translation or imitate a source's prose.
- Do not replace a consequential researcher checkpoint with an agent decision.
- Do not end a session without canonical state, a stopping condition for open-ended work, and an exact next action.
